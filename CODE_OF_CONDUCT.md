# Code of Conduct

Research Ledger is intended for researchers, writers, engineers, reviewers, and
students who care about verifiable AI-assisted research workflows.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and specific.
- Critique ideas, designs, evidence, and code rather than people.
- Assume mistakes are possible in research workflows, documentation, and code.
- Keep legal, academic, and security claims precise.
- Respect privacy when discussing research notes, sources, drafts, or audit
  bundles.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, personal attacks, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing another person's private research material, credentials, keys, or
  identifying details without permission.
- Posting exploit details for unresolved vulnerabilities in public issues.
- Repeatedly derailing technical or methodological discussions after maintainers
  have asked to refocus.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to project repositories, issues, pull requests,
review discussions, release discussions, and project-managed community spaces.

## Reporting

For conduct concerns, contact the maintainers through the repository's private
security or maintainer contact channel when available. For security issues, use
the process in `SECURITY.md` and avoid posting exploit details publicly.

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues, restrict participation, or block
accounts when necessary to protect the project community.
